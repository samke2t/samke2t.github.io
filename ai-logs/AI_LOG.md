# AI-Assisted Work Log

Project: Then & Now: Digital Object Museum of Generational Memory  
Coursework: CPT208 Human-Centric Computing  
Team: B4-4  

This log records how AI tools were used to support the design, writing, prototyping, and implementation of our project. AI was used as an assistant for brainstorming, language refinement, structure checking, and coding support. Final decisions, design direction, research interpretation, prototype selection, and evaluation conclusions were reviewed and edited by the team.

## Summary of AI Use

| AI tool | Main purpose | Project area supported | Human checking and editing |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ChatGPT | Idea generation, wording refinement, research synthesis support | Research, personas, design rationale, evaluation writing, references section | Team members checked the output against our actual project direction, removed unsuitable content, and rewrote text to match our own design decisions. |
| Cursor | Code generation and debugging support | Portfolio website structure, HTML/CSS layout, JavaScript interaction, responsive design | Generated code was manually reviewed, tested in browser, and edited to fit the existing visual style and content. |
| Figma Make | High-fidelity prototype exploration | Mobile prototype screens, interaction ideas, visual layout experiments | The team selected useful parts, adjusted screens manually, and aligned the prototype with our final concept. |

## Example Prompts Used

1. "Help us brainstorm HCI project ideas about intergenerational family connection and heritage."
2. "Summarize these research notes into strengths, limitations, and design implications for our project."
3. "Rewrite this paragraph in a clearer portfolio style for a human-centric computing coursework."
4. "Generate Crazy 8 ideas for a mobile app that helps grandparents and grandchildren share memories through objects."
5. "Help explain a React + Spring Boot + MySQL architecture for a student portfolio website."
6. "Turn our usability testing observations into concise evaluation findings and design implications."

## How We Controlled AI Use

- AI-generated text was not copied directly without review.
- Research claims were checked against the papers and products we referenced.
- Design decisions were made by the team after discussion, not by AI alone.
- Prototype screens and website sections were manually edited to match our concept, users, and visual style.
- Evaluation results were based on our own user testing observations.
- AI was used mainly to improve efficiency, structure, wording, and code debugging.

## Reflection on AI Support

AI was useful for quickly generating alternatives, improving English expression, and helping us organize complex design material into a clearer portfolio structure. It helped us compare different design directions and explain why the object comparison exhibition was more suitable than a game-like or voice-only concept.

However, AI outputs were sometimes too general. For example, early suggestions focused on broad family communication rather than our specific heritage object museum idea. Some generated wording also sounded too promotional or did not match our actual prototype. Because of this, the team had to check, shorten, and rewrite the content carefully.

Overall, AI supported the project as a drafting and reflection tool, while the final concept, user research interpretation, prototype selection, website content, and evaluation conclusions were completed and validated by the team.
