01

Research

"

The problem is not a lack of care, but a lack of shared language and shared topics between generations.

"
Motivation Ref. 01

We focus on Topic B4: the silent connection gap between generations. Genuine care exists across families, but shared topics, interaction rhythms, and emotional expression are often misaligned. Grandparents and grandchildren differ in digital literacy, memory habits, and communication preferences, making everyday interaction awkward and less frequent. Family memories such as old photos and oral stories often stay static and rarely spark children's interest. Family Link / Then & Now turns these memories into playful, shared experiences that lower barriers for elders and motivate younger users through puzzles and story-unlocking.

Research Gap Ref. 02

Existing academic studies mainly focus on long-distance intergenerational connection or game-based approaches to bridge generations. Commercial products mostly support photo archiving or remote messaging, but rarely promote in-person, object-centered, and playful interaction around real family memories. There is a clear gap in solutions that balance elders' low-learning, simple operation with grandchildren's gamified engagement, while using tangible everyday objects to start natural conversations and create new shared memories at home.

A Academic Papers Review

Paper 1

Reis, Mercer & Boger

Technologies for fostering intergenerational connectivity and relationships

Strengths
  • Maps existing intergenerational connection technologies.
  • Shows that this is a valid HCI research area.
  • Emphasizes meaningful cross-generation interaction.
Limitations
  • Limited focus on in-person family settings.
  • Lacks object-centered interaction design.
  • Does not address unequal digital literacy.
Design implication

Use everyday objects as low-pressure conversation starters, rather than relying only on game-based interaction.

Paper 3

Choe

Tangible Memories and Elders: Objects as Containers, Reminders and Instruments

Strengths
  • Defines objects as carriers of personal memory.
  • Shows how elders use objects for remembering.
  • Highlights the need for simple, familiar tools.
Limitations
  • Focuses on individual memory support.
  • Does not explore intergenerational interaction.
  • Lacks playful engagement for younger users.
Design implication

Make everyday objects the main interaction trigger, with a simple visual interface for elders and engaging content for grandchildren.

Paper 2

Li, Hu, Hengeveld & Hummels

Supporting intergenerational memento storytelling for older adults through a tangible display

Strengths
  • Shows mementoes as emotional anchors.
  • Identifies elders' difficulty sharing keepsakes digitally.
  • Supports tangible displays for elder-led storytelling.
Limitations
  • Limited focus on playful shared interaction.
  • Less attention to face-to-face family gatherings.
  • Focuses more on formal mementoes than everyday objects.
Design implication

Use familiar objects such as CDs, photos, or letters to naturally trigger in-person storytelling.

Paper 4

Xu, Gielen & van der Vegte

A Framework for Digital Technology to Foster Intergenerational Bonds at Home

Strengths
  • Highlights the lack of research on in-person family bonding.
  • Focuses on grandparent-grandchild relationships at home.
  • Shows the importance of everyday family gatherings.
Limitations
  • Does not use everyday objects as interaction triggers.
  • Does not reduce awkwardness in family conversations.
  • Lacks a lightweight design for different digital abilities.
Design implication

Focus on home-based, face-to-face bonding through object-centered, low-pressure interaction.

B Commercial Products Review

Product 1

Google Photos

Strengths
  • Organizes family photos automatically.
  • Supports shared family albums.
  • Simple and familiar interface.
Limitations
  • Focuses on passive photo storage.
  • Does not trigger in-person conversation.
  • Lacks playful family interaction.
Design Insight

Turn shared memories into object-based, in-person activities instead of passive archives.

Product 3

StoryCorps

Strengths
  • Guides meaningful story recording.
  • Values elder voices and memories.
  • Provides useful conversation prompts.
Limitations
  • Feels formal and archive-focused.
  • Lacks playful casual interaction.
  • Does not use physical objects.
Design Insight

Combine storytelling prompts with light, playful, object-based interaction.

Product 2

Marco Polo

Strengths
  • Supports low-pressure video sharing.
  • Reduces pressure from live calls.
  • Creates warm family communication.
Limitations
  • Designed mainly for remote contact.
  • Lacks physical memory prompts.
  • Limited co-present interaction.
Design Insight

Adapt low-pressure communication into face-to-face object-based family activities.

Product 4

FamilyAlbum

Strengths
  • Provides private family sharing.
  • Simple for older users.
  • Organizes memories in timelines.
Limitations
  • Supports static browsing only.
  • Does not start conversations.
  • Lacks object-triggered interaction.
Design Insight

Keep the private, simple environment, but add interactive memory activities.

C Stakeholders

Who Family Link is Designed For — Family Link focuses on relationship maintenance during in-person family gatherings, not just individual messaging behaviour. It is designed for both users and the connection between them.

Primary

Grandparents

Needs
  • Want to share family memories with loved ones.
  • Prefer simple and familiar tools.
  • May feel unsure using complex apps.
Design Response

Familiar objects and low-input interaction help grandparents share stories without technical pressure.

Primary

Grandchildren

Needs
  • Prefer playful and interactive experiences.
  • May find passive storytelling boring.
  • Need easier ways to start conversations.
Design Response

Object comparison turns family memories into a playful activity, making storytelling natural and engaging.

Relational

Grandparent–Grandchild Relationship

Needs
  • Needs shared topics during family gatherings.
  • Often affected by different digital habits.
  • Requires low-pressure emotional connection.
Design Response

Everyday objects serve as bridges, creating warm face-to-face interaction between generations.

Secondary

Parents & Wider Family

Needs
  • Want closer family relationships.
  • Often act as facilitators during gatherings.
  • Need a flexible tool for different contexts.
Design Response

Supports family bonding and can extend to more relationships and shared events over time.
