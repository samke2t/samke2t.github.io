Everyday objects carry the traces of how people lived, cared, learned, and remembered. Then & Now invites families to place objects from different generations side by side, turning small comparisons into a shared exhibition of memory.

01

Research

Motivation Ref. 01

The problem is not a lack of care, but a lack of shared language and shared topics between generations. Modern digital life moves at a pace that often leaves older generations behind, while younger members lose touch with the tangible history of their families.

Research Gap Ref. 02

Most existing solutions are either "Digital Shoe-boxes" (static storage) or "Social Media Streams" (ephemeral updates). There is a significant gap in facilitated intergenerational dialogue that uses physical artifacts as anchors for storytelling.

"The problem is not a lack of care, but a lack of shared language and shared topics between generations."
